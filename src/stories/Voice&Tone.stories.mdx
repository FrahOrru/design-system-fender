# Voice & Tone

<div className="subheading">Fender Voice & Tone</div>

The Fender Voice is how we sound in writing or out loud.

The characteristics of the Fender Voice are:

<ul>
  <li>Expertise</li>
  <li>Rock & Roll</li>
  <li>Young</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Tone has Four Parts</div>

The four Qualities of Fender tone are the following, 
it will change according to which Audience we are speaking with.

<ul>
  <li>Exciting</li>
  <li>Promising</li>
  <li>Knowledgeable</li>
  <li>Dedicated</li>
</ul>


<div className="subheading">Expertise - Remember every Journey is different</div>

Create content that will help our users enjoy their musical instruments for a lifetime.

<ul>
  <li>Explain in simple terms </li>
  <li>Tailor your knowledge to the user</li>
  <li>Appreciate the discovery process</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Rock & Roll - Connect users with our Spirit</div>

Rock & Roll is a spirit, keep in mind that we are coming together to celebrate Rock & Roll. 
Speak to include anyone in this spirit.

<ul>
  <li>Be welcoming when introducing a new user</li>
  <li>Rock & Roll welcomes Diversity</li>
  <li>Stay true to Rock & roll</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Supporting - Building instruments for Humans</div>

Building and receiving an instrument is a big deal.
Celebrate the Emotion involved.

<ul>
  <li>Its exciting to receive a new instrument</li>
  <li>Show consideration to Customers</li>
  <li>Show understanding of the emotion involved</li>
</ul>

<hr />

<div className="subheading">Tonal Attributes</div>

Tone is used to accustom ourselves to different user’s and there situations. 

We want our users to feel
<ul>
  <li>Guided</li>
  <li>At ease</li>
  <li>Trust</li>
  <li>Confident</li>
</ul>

To do that we use the following Tonal Attributes

<ul>
  <li>Knowledgeable</li>
  <li>Assuring</li>
  <li>Exciting</li>
  <li>Dedicated</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Tonal Qualities</div>

<b>Knowledgeable</b>

You are focused on the users interest,
gently assisting with making decisions that require experience.

<ul>
  <li>Be engaging & inquisitive</li>
  <li>Be friendly & guiding</li>
</ul>

Guiding someone who has a specific Idea can be annoying,
in this case less is more.

<ul>
  <li>Don’t be condescending </li>
  <li>Don’t saturate content with jargon and detail</li>
</ul>

<b>Promising</b>

Helping users to arrive at a decision, requires time & trust.
Take the time to help the user make a decision.

<ul>
  <li>Do be helpful & Encouraging</li>
  <li>Use words like “we”, “let’s” </li>
</ul>

Be realistic and manage user expectations

<ul>
  <li>Don’t be passive (making the user tell you what they need)</li>
  <li>Don’t use language that is over-familiar to convey a false sense of warmth </li>
</ul>

<b>Exciting</b>

We make musicians, it is an exciting process to discover and learn.

<ul>
  <li>Do celebrate success of our musicians</li>
  <li>Do enjoy each step</li>
</ul>

Avoid “hyping” every step of the journey

<ul>
  <li>Don’t make the entire process a dopamine ride</li>
  <li>Don’t use phrases like “wow!” “thats exciting!!!!!!”</li>
</ul>

<b>Dedicated</b>

We take one step at a time, progress over perfection

<ul>
  <li>Do show interest in achieving the users goal</li>
  <li>Do show understanding for the users motivation</li>
</ul>

Dont force the user to complete the journey

<ul>
  <li>Don’t make it about the end of the user journey</li>
  <li>Don’t use language that feels forceful</li>
</ul>
