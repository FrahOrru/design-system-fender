# Content Guidelines

What to pay attention to:

The tone is appropriate for the scenario

<ul>
  <li>A win or success is celebrated with excitement</li>
  <li>A stressful scenario is managed with consideration and a knowledgeable tone</li>
  <li>The content sounds like a Rock & Roll Grandad that knows what key you play without ever having heard you play</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Imagery</div>

<ul>
  <li>
  Use imagery that is relevant to what the user is looking for
    <ul>
      <li>For example, when a user visits the tune section of our page, show info graphics explaining the tuning process</li>
      <li>Show video content with thumbnails of real people</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Reading</div>

<ul>
  <li>Use common words, the longer you’r words the more likely the reader is going to skip them. </li>
  <li>Short sentences, people with some learning disabilities read letter for letter. They cannot fully understand a sentence if it’s too long</li>
  <li>Minimise the use of Jargon, plain english works best.</li>
</ul>

<div className="subheading">Know your Audience</div>

<ul>
  <li>
  Always keep in mind who you are writing for, a visitor to the beginners section is likely to feel overwhelmed.
    <ul>
      <li>Minimise the information overload</li>
      <li>Use Clear titles</li>
      <li>Imagery</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Aim to answer the most important questions first</li>
</ul>
